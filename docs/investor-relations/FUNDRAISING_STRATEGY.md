# Fundraising Strategy (SAFE vs Priced)

## Decision Summary

Based on the current plan in Investor Relations (targeting a $20-30M Series A),
this should be a priced round. A SAFE is more appropriate for small bridge or
pre-seed raises where speed matters more than valuation clarity.

## SAFE vs Priced Round

### Use a SAFE if:

- Raising a small bridge or pre-seed ($0.5M to $3M)
- You need speed and lower legal overhead
- You do not yet have reliable revenue benchmarks

### Use a Priced Round if:

- Raising $10M+ from institutional investors
- You have stable revenue and repeatable GTM
- You need clear governance, preferences, and a clean cap table

## Recommended Path (Current Plan)

- Round type: Priced Series A
- Target raise: $20M to $30M
- Terms: 1x non-participating preference, standard pro rata
- Option pool: 10% to 15% post-money (set by lead investor requirements)

## Inputs Needed to Finalize

- Target pre-money valuation or range
- Exact raise amount
- Current cap table and any SAFEs/notes
- Option pool requirement from investors
